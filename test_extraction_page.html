Four Levels of Protein Structure

  • Primary structure: The sequence of amino acids
  • Secondary structure: Alpha helices and beta sheets
  • Tertiary structure: 3D folding with disulfide bridges
  • Quaternary structure: Multiple polypeptide chains

Important Terms

Disulfide bridge: Covalent bond between cysteine residues

Denaturation: Loss of protein structure

Enzyme kinetics: Study of enzyme reaction rates

Michaelis-Menten: Model for enzyme kinetics

Key Concepts

Chaperonins help with protein folding. Alpha-helix and beta-turn are secondary structures.
